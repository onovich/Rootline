# ROOTLINE: Underground Root Civilization CRPG

## Game Design Document

## 1. World Overview

A subterranean civilization exists inside a root-like cave network.
Human societies evolved into isolated factions defined by belief
systems, survival logic, and spatial constraints.

Caves are not geography---they are topology.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 2. Core Fantasy

-   You are a cave cartographer / observer / intervention agent
-   You influence civilization by altering cave connectivity
-   Every connection reshapes culture, economy, and survival

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 3. Factions (Examples)

### Echo Cult

-   Belief: caves store memory
-   taboo: destroying walls
-   communication: resonance signals

### Excavator Union

-   Belief: expansion equals survival
-   behavior: aggressive tunneling

### Silent Order

-   Belief: movement corrupts stability
-   behavior: isolationist

### Strata Guild

-   Belief: structural stability is sacred
-   focus: collapse prevention

### Mycelium Collective

-   Belief: humans are part of underground ecology
-   behavior: symbiosis

### Machine Remnants

-   Belief: old world machines are divine artifacts

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 4. Player Role

Primary role: - Cave Surveyor / Mapper / Intervention Agent

Abilities: - connect caves - sever connections - mediate factions -
explore unknown nodes

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 5. Core Gameplay Loop

1.  Explore cave node
2.  Encounter faction
3.  Understand belief system
4.  Modify topology
5.  Observe civilization change
6.  Generate emergent history

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 6. Combat System

-   Squad-based control
-   RimWorld-style AI combat
-   Terrain = tactical structure
-   Line-of-sight via graph connectivity
-   Environmental collapse as weapon/system

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 7. Resource Systems

Three axes:

Survival: - food - oxygen - heat

Social: - trust - influence - cohesion

Spatial: - connectivity - stability - accessibility

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 8. Quest System

Quests are not scripted, but state transformations:

Examples: - connect isolated factions - prevent belief spread - ensure
survival of node cluster - trigger migration chain

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 9. Narrative Structure

Three layers: 1. NPC personal stories 2. faction history evolution 3.
cave topology history

Narrative emerges from simulation.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 10. AI Content Pipeline

Step 1: AI generates world graph Step 2: AI generates factions + beliefs
Step 3: Codex simulates world evolution Step 4: filter playable seeds
Step 5: extract narrative arcs

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 11. Tone & Aesthetic

-   realistic sci-fi underground survival
-   grounded human behavior
-   subtle mysticism layered into systems
-   inspired by GRRM-style historical depth and CRPG narrative tone

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 12. Win Condition

No strict win condition: - survival - influence - narrative emergence -
systemic evolution
